Jack McKenna

I am a mathematical physicist based in London. I am a lecturer at City St George's, University of London. My research is in the field of topological solitons. In particular, I look at low-dimensional models where fermions are coupled to the soliton field.

I am a Visiting Lecturer at City St George's, University of London. I am primarily engaged on the Mathematics Foundation Programme, on which I am a lecturer, tutor and Personal Tutor. I also lecture on the undergraduate mathematics programme. I am an organiser of the Maths Learning Café, a thrice-weekly event series where we run a relaxed space where students can drop in for a chat with their lecturers, get help with assignments, or just pick up a hot drink and a light snack. The goals of this event series are to:

  • Arrange an alternative and attractive setting for Lecturers’ office hours, which we know are underutilised!
  • Provide some food to students who come to classes without eating in the morning.
  • Help foster a sense of community, both with the faculty and with each other, for students in all stages.
  • Ultimately, aid progression by encouraging students to work on their assignments on campus and take up the support we offer.

I did my Ph.D. in Mathematics at the School of Mathematics, Statistics and Actuarial Science in the University of Kent. I was supervised by Steffen Krusch. I have an M.A. in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge. I have a Graduate Diploma in Mathematics, and an M.Sc. in Theoretical Physics, from King's College London (University of London). At KCL, I wrote a graduate dissertation on Monopoles and the Mathematics of Gauge Theory for the Graduate Diploma, and a Master's thesis on Chern-Simons Theory as a Topological Quantum Field Theory for the M.Sc.

I offer private tutoring in mathematics or theoretical physics subjects to undergraduate students in London, as well as A-level tutoring in mathematics. If you are interested, please get in touch at the e-mail address given below.

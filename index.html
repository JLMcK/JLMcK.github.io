I am a Ph.D. student in mathematical physics at the School of Mathematics, Statistics and Actuarial Science in the University of Kent. I am supervised by Steffen Krusch. My research is in the field of topological solitons. In particular, I look at low-dimensional models where fermions are coupled to the soliton field.

I have an M.A. in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge. I have a Graduate Diploma in Mathematics, and an M.Sc. in Theoretical Physics, from King's College London (University of London). At KCL, I wrote a graduate dissertation on Monopoles and the Mathematics of Gauge Theory for the Graduate Diploma, and a Master's thesis on Chern-Simons Theory as a Topological Quantum Field Theory for the M.Sc.

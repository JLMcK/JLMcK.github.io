Jack McKenna

I am a mathematical physicist based in London. My research is in the field of topological solitons. In particular, I look at low-dimensional models where fermions are coupled to the soliton field.

I did my Ph.D. in Mathematics at the School of Mathematics, Statistics and Actuarial Science in the University of Kent. I was supervised by Steffen Krusch. I have an M.A. in Mathematics from the University of Cambridge. I have a Graduate Diploma in Mathematics, and an M.Sc. in Theoretical Physics, from King's College London (University of London). At KCL, I wrote a graduate dissertation on Monopoles and the Mathematics of Gauge Theory for the Graduate Diploma, and a Master's thesis on Chern-Simons Theory as a Topological Quantum Field Theory for the M.Sc.

From October 2023 I will be a Visiting Lecturer on the Mathematics Foundation Programme at City University of London. I am also a part-time Graduate Teaching Assistant at King's College London. I offer private tutoring in mathematics or theoretical physics subjects to undergraduate students in London, as well as A-level tutoring in mathematics.
